Walk Amongst the Trees

Before the 1987 storm when many of the trees were blown down, I once spent a warm spring night sleeping alone among the mysterious denizens of Chanctonbury Ring. Back then, I was a keen “wild walker” and had been hiking the South Downs Way, sleeping rough as I went. I can’t pretend to have conversed with my newfound friends in fluent Ogham, the Celtic language of trees, but they certainly haunted my dreams and left a profound impression on me.
